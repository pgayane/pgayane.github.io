October 2013 - Computer Science 101

School N27, Gyumri, Armenia
This was a two-week introduction course to computer science which intention was to familiarise high-school students with programming. The idea was to give them a chance to feel what is programming before they will choose their future profession. Besides that I wanted to show that internet is full of useful information. I chose the Stanford's Computer Science 101 online course and translated the first 2 week material into Armenian. It requires no previous background in programming and explains concepts through working with pictures. Transforming pictures and applying color effects by writing a few lines of code are truly exciting, not only for students but also for a graduate student of Computer Science like me. In the end of the course we also visited a Software Development company so that students can picture the future of being a programmer. Hopefully in future I will be able to continue and extend this project.

You can read more about it here and the website with all the materials will be online in near future.

June 2012 - Introduction to NLP

Luys Knowledge Fair, Ayb school, Yerevan, Armenia and IT school, Gyumri, Armenia
These two-day seminars in Gyumri and Yerevan were part of Luys Develop Armenia Program. The goal was to introduce Natural Language Processing(NLP) to Armenian students. It was short introduction answering the questions of what is NLP, why is it hard, what can be done using NLP and a little bit on how it works.

For more information see day 1 presentation and day 2 presentation.

July 2012 - History of Computers

Women Employment Centre, Gyumri, Armenia
This was a non-that-much-technical talk for mid-age women who struggle to find a job after having kids. The talk was mainly a sequence of anecdotes in the History of Computers and Programming, such as why bugs are called bugs or allegories describing Computer Science concepts such as a language we create when we were kids as a allegory to encryption.
